bpp.architecture.surface module#

class bpp.architecture.surface.Surface(id='0ff14e7e-aa95-44f7-a8ba-8adc0bfcd217')#

Bases: object

Represents a surface in a building energy model.

A surface can be a wall, roof/ceiling, or floor. It has attributes such as type, construction, boundary condition, sun exposure, wind exposure, and coordinates. It also has methods to calculate its azimuth and height, and to write itself to an OpenStudio model.

calculate_azimuth()#

Calculates the azimuth of the surface.

The azimuth is the angle between the north direction and the direction towards which the surface is facing, measured clockwise. The method assumes that the vertices of the surface are ordered counterclockwise when viewed from the outside of the surface.

Returns:
float

The azimuth of the surface in degrees, relative to true north.

calculate_height()#

Calculates the height of the surface.

The height is calculated as the difference between the maximum and minimum z-values of the surface’s coordinates.

Returns:
float

The height of the surface.

lookup_default_construction()#

Assigns a default construction to the surface based on its type and boundary condition.

This method checks if the surface already has a construction assigned to it. If not, it assigns a default construction based on the surface’s type (Wall, Roof/Ceiling, Floor) and boundary condition (Exterior, Ground, Adiabatic, Matched). The default constructions are retrieved from the project’s default construction set.

Returns:
None
set_values(input_values)#

Updates the attributes of the Surface object based on the provided input values.

This method takes a dictionary of attribute-value pairs and updates the Surface object’s attributes accordingly. If an attribute in the input_values dictionary does not exist in the Surface object, it will be added to the object.

Parameters:
